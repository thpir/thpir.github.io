Experience

My Energy Desk app

My Energy Desk is an application I wrote from scratch. It's an energy meter logger tool designed for people who want to log and monitor their energy consumption at home on a monthly or yearly basis. There are four types of energy that you can keep track of: electricity consumption, natural gas consumption, water consumption and grid injection (energy generated at home and injected into the grid). By entering your meter readings periodically, you will build a database containing your meter readings. That data is used to build graphs of your usage over the years. After some time you will be able to know how your energy usage changes over the years, and be able to take action if needed. The app is 100% free of charge, advertisement-free and you can export your database to Excel at any time! The application can be downloaded from the play store. This was the first time I have released an app on the Google play store. This project was also a great exercise to learn to work with databases.

Tags: Java, Android Studio, SQLite, PhilJay/MPAndroidChart, Play Store

Serial Communicator app

This application is developed to send or control an arduino with your mobile phone by sending commands over serial communication. In theory this application could be used with other serial devices as well. Simply connect your smartphone to the Arduino with a USB-cable, scan for devices, and connect to the microcontroller. In a textfield the user can enter single characters, numbers, or any other data. When the send command is given, the application converts it to a byteArray and sends it over to the Arduino. As soon as the connection has been establised the application periodically "listens" for any data send from the serial device to the smartphone. I've included a very simple arduino code to test the application. The code is designed to control an LED by sending the "x" or "o" character.

Tags: Kotlin, Android Studio, Serial Communication, felHR85/UsbSerial, Arduino

APOD app

I've always been fascinated by space. When I learned about API's I found that there was an API from nasa that releases one picture (or video) about space every single day! NASA's Astronomy Picture Of the Day API. This API was the perfect one to start playing around with get-requests in android, learn to handle JSON files, etc... The application is quite simple: When opening the app, the Astronomy Picture Of the Day will be loaded on the screen. I choose for the option to fill the screen although, for some pictures that will not be the best option. If you want more information about the picture, or you want it in it's original size without any parts cut of to fit the full screen of your phone, just tab the info button and you will be redirected to a new activity where you can see the picture in it's original size, along with the title and a description.

Tags: Kotlin, Android Studio, APOD, NASA, API,

Stand Up app

An Android application that notifies the user to stand up and move around at specific intervals (15 minutes, 30 minutes, 1 hour or 2 hours). The user can choose a time frame during the day in which he/she wants to get notified to move around and stretch. The user can also choose to be notified only during the working days. This project was a fun side project to practice on delivering notifications at a specific interval, which I needed for my other app: My Energy Desk.
